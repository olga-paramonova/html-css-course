History

The Local County Zoo, a nonprofit located in Saint Charles, Illinois, was established in May 1937 by Conner Willard. That spring, the Saint Charles Zoological Society was formed to work with the park board and to increase interest in the Zoo.
The Zoo’s first animals were donated by Conner himself. After that, many organizations throughout Illinois donated animals such as badgers, foxes, and mountain goats to add to the collection. In 1939, monkeys were added, the first species native to areas outside the United States. Currently, the Zoo cares for approximately 75 different species of animals from USA and around the world.
The Local County Zoo is one of only 225 zoos accredited by the Association of Zoos and Aquariums. Each year, more than 125,000 guests visit the Local County Zoo’s 28-acre park on the shores of Lake Michigan.

Exhibits

African

Watch elephants, giraffes, zebras, and ostriches roam on a 2-acre reproduction of an Africa savanna, and observe lions and baboons in their separate natural habitat exhibits.

Asian

Various endangered and vulnerable Asian species are housed at Riverbanks including tigers, babirusas, siamangs, a tapir, and Komodo dragons.

Australian

Australian Exhibit is home to koalas, Australian fish and reptiles. If you like the sound of mingling with the marsupials, be sure to check out Kangaroo Walkabout — an interactive walk-through exhibit featuring red kangaroos and wallabies!

North American

Guests are greeted by North American river otters with broad under- and above-water views of swimming otters as well as unparalleled, shared-boundary views of neighboring grizzly bears, california sea lions and a harbor seal.

Aquarium

Amphibians, fish, and invertebrates are on display in four galleries: South Carolina, the Desert, the Tropics, and the Ocean—featuring a 55,000-gallon Pacific coral reef tank with sharks, moray eels, and more.

Bird House

The Birdhouse features three distinctive, geographically-themed bird habitats: Penguin Coast, Asian Trek, and Savanna Camp, showcasing the birds of the arid African and South American savannas.

Farmyard

The all-new Farmyard provides a bigger, better farm experience! Now guests of all ages can meet new farm animals and enjoy up-close encounters at interactive walk-in yard.

Reptile Complex

Open year-round, the gallery showcases the best of what Reptile Complex has to offer: turtles, lizards, snakes, frogs, and crocodiles from around the world, all in one place.
